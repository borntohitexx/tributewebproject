Stan Lee [1922 - 2018]

Stanley Martin "Stan Lee" Lieber is a comic book writer, editor, and publisher for the popular Marvel Comics series leading to the creation of America's most popular superheroes such as Spider-Man, Iron Man, Thor, and the Hulk.

He was brought into Marvel Comics (then known as Timely Comics) by his uncle. His career began as an office assistant and editor, but he always dreamed of becoming a writer and writing a ‘Great American Novel’. As a comic book writer, he was very focused on ensuring that the heroes he created felt real. He wanted people to be able to relate to the strengths and weakness of each character.

His development of the Marvel Cinematic Universe ran counter to DC’s superheroes. At the time, DC was the more popular brand. However, under his direction, he pushed new unique and creative characters that addressed more serious themes to appeal to readers of all ages.

Aside from publishing comics for sale, he assisted with publishing comics surrounding the themes of drug use, racism, and discrimination. He also set up the “Stan Lee” foundation to improve access to literacy, culture, and arts. His contribution to the fictional world as well as bringing to light the issues of bigotry and inspiring kids to be heroes themselves makes him a real-life superhero. His legacy continues to live on in his creations.

Timeline

  • 1922 - Born in Manhattan, New York City to Jewish immigrant parents
  • 1939 - Graduated from high school and landed an assistant job at Timely Comics with help of his uncle
  • 1941 - Became interim editor, adopted the pseudonym “Stan Lee” (to hide the low social status of comics), and co-created the Destroyer, Jack Frost, Father Time
  • 1942 - Joined the US Army as a member of the Signal Corps and continued to aid in writing comics for Timely while in the Army
  • 1945 - Returns from World War II
  • 1947 - Marries his wife Joan Boocock
  • 1951 - Timely Comics rebrands as Atlas comics
  • 1960 - Wanted to quit writing comics as he felt the career becoming bland, but his wife ushered him to write the stories he wanted
  • 1961 - In an effort to re-invent his career and portray superheroes in a more realistic light, he created the Fantastic Four
  • 1962 - Creates the now famous Spiderman, Hulk, and Thor
  • 1963 - Creation of the X-men team
  • 1971 - Writes a story on the bad-effects of drugs starring Spiderman
  • 1972 - Assumes the role of publisher as he claimed the president role was too technical for himself
  • 1981 - Moved from New York to California to set up an animation studio and become more invested in Marvel TV and movie projects
  • 1998 - Co-founded Stan Lee media for web-based superhero animations and content
  • 2000 - Stan Lee Media became bankrupt and co-founder Peter Paul and corporate officer Stephen Gordon was found guilty of illegal stock manipulation. Lee was not involved
  • 2001 - Co-founded POW! Entertainment for film, television, and video game properties
  • 2008 - Awarded the National Medal of Arts by former US president George W. Bush
  • 2010 - Created the Stan Lee Foundation that contributed support programs towards literacy, education, and arts
  • 2011 - Receives Star on Hollywood Walk of Fame
  • 2018 - Passes away due to pneumonia
